CHART PATTERNS
Double Top BEARISH
Two peaks at nearly the same price, forming an 'M' shape. Buyers failed to push higher on the second attempt — a reversal signal.

Entry: Short on a confirmed break below the neckline (the trough between the two peaks).
Stop: Above the second peak.
Target: Distance from peak to neckline, projected down from the breakout.
Double Bottom BULLISH
Two troughs at nearly the same price, forming a 'W' shape. Sellers couldn't push price lower the second time — a reversal signal.

Entry: Long on a confirmed break above the neckline.
Stop: Below the second trough.
Target: Depth of the pattern projected upward.
Head & Shoulders BEARISH
Three peaks — a higher middle peak (head) flanked by two lower peaks (shoulders) at similar heights. Classic topping pattern.

Entry: Short on neckline break.
Target: Head-to-neckline distance projected downward.
Inv. Head & Shoulders BULLISH
The mirror of H&S — three troughs with a deeper middle. Signals the end of a downtrend as sellers lose control.

Entry: Long on neckline break.
Stop: Below the right shoulder.
Bull Flag BULLISH
A sharp upward move (the pole) followed by a tight, slightly downward-drifting channel (the flag). A healthy pause before the trend continues.

Entry: Long on breakout above the upper flag boundary.
Target: Add the pole length to the breakout point.
Bear Flag BEARISH
A sharp downward move followed by a slight upward drift (the relief rally). Sellers regrouping before resuming the downtrend.

Entry: Short on breakdown below the lower flag boundary.
Rising Wedge BEARISH
Both highs and lows are rising but converging — buyers are losing momentum as the range narrows. Despite looking bullish, this is a reversal warning.

Entry: Short on break below the lower trendline.
Best at: Key resistance levels.
Falling Wedge BULLISH
Both highs and lows falling but converging — sellers losing steam as the range narrows. A bullish reversal or continuation signal.

Entry: Long on break above the upper trendline.
INDICATORS
RSI (14) MOMENTUM
Relative Strength Index. Measures the speed of recent price moves on a 0–100 scale.

Above 70 — overbought, watch for reversal down.
Below 30 — oversold, watch for reversal up.
50 line — momentum is neutral.

Pro tip: RSI divergence (price makes a new high but RSI doesn't) is one of the most reliable early reversal signals available.
EMA 20 TREND
The 20-period Exponential Moving Average. Reacts faster than a simple MA because it weights recent candles more heavily. Acts as a short-term dynamic support/resistance line.

Use: In an uptrend, price bouncing off the EMA 20 is a continuation signal. A clean break below it suggests the short-term trend is changing.
EMA 50 TREND
The medium-term trend filter. Widely watched by institutions and retail traders alike.

Price above EMA 50 = bullish bias.
EMA 20 crossing above EMA 50 = golden cross (bullish).
EMA 20 crossing below EMA 50 = death cross (bearish).

Combine EMA crossovers with pattern signals for the highest-confidence setups.
